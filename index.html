Young Minds Library Project is a youth-led non-profit initiative founded by a 14-year-old brother and his 11-year-old sister, and run by student volunteers. We create classroom libraries and run reading programs for underprivileged children in rural Andhra Pradesh.

Our Mission

To empower young minds in rural India through the magic of reading — encouraging literacy, creativity, and confidence. We believe every child deserves access to books — no fines, no barriers, no discrimination.

What We Do

Classroom Libraries

  • Books available: 3,300+
  • Languages: English & Telugu
  • Age group: 8–15 years

Reading Programs

  • Read-Aloud Sessions
  • Readathons & Rewards
  • Book lending: ~2 books per student per week

Community Engagement

Led by student volunteers and trained library leaders to make reading fun and inclusive.

Impact

  • 3 schools in Andhra Pradesh
  • 23 classroom libraries
  • 1,041 students served
  • 3,300 books catalogued
  • 67 student volunteers
  • Average monthly footfall: 180 readers

Goal: Expand to 10 schools and build 50+ classroom libraries.
